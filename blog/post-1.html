First Blog! Who I Am and Why CS.

Hello! Welcome to my blog. My name is Jelani Beaugris. I am a junior at Rutgers University majoring in Computer Science. Outside of that, I am Haitain-African American, an older sibling, and have a strong passion for calisthenics. This first blog centers around computer science and why I chose to pursue it!

Why Computer Science

I first picked up Computer Science in my junior year of high school. I was struck by the idea of being able to create software and automate certain task at will but, the most interesting part for me was the ability to build whatever you wished to build. You see, when I was a kid, I was always curious but more than that, was always looking to both creative but complex task. I loved math, loved to build and create things, was (and still am) a huge fan of legos, and my first ever dream career was to actually be an architect. However, that passion shaped into something new when I picked up python for my first time in junior year of high school, and the rest could be considered history. I tend to get bored of things really fast but, computer science offers endless opportunities to learn, given the extremely fast advancement in the field, as well as the many unanswered questions within the field as well. Lastly, computer science is challenging, but I find my life would be fairly boring without challenges. My mom used to tell me about how I'd complain to her in elementary school that my classes were too easy, so whenever I come to her now about the exciting but difficult content im tackling everyday, she usually laughs it off and tells me "well at least it's better than you being bored, if it were easy you wouldn't enjoy it."

What I'm Working On

Currently, I'm working on a project to build a course recommender system using AI agents. I'm also researching how to effectively elicit preferences from users using AI agents and figuring out the limitations of current approaches. It's a very hard project, a lot of nuances and complexities involved, especially when it comes to deciding baselines and finding reliable methods in a specific application of the field of study where the data itself and provenly working methods are sparse. I do enjoy every second of it though! Outside of that, I consistently work on improving my skills and knowledge in the field of computer science, whether that be through taking challenging courses at Rutgers University, solving coding problems on leetcode, or reading up on papers related to AI or my current research. I'm always looking to improve myself intellectually.
